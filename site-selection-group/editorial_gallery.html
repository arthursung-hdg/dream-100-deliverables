The Thinking Behind These Creatives

Our Approach

We don't point one ad at everyone and hope. Each creative is built for a different slice of your market, sorted by how aware that person already is of the problem, so the message meets them exactly where they are. Here is how the five fit together.

Reaching Your Whole Market

Problem Unaware

Leaders whose center is underperforming but who have not yet traced it to the labor pool. Ad 01, When The Talent Runs Out makes the hidden labor-depth risk concrete and gets them to look.

Problem Aware

Leaders already living a specific pain. Ad 02, Re-Hiring The Same Seats speaks to the attrition treadmill; Ad 03, When The Savings Vanish speaks to the wage inflation eroding their business case.

Solution Aware

Leaders actively deciding how to choose. Ad 04, A Million-Dollar Guess reaches those weighing onshore, nearshore, and offshore; Ad 05, Whose Side Is Your Advisor On reaches those questioning conflicted broker advice.

Why One Pain Per Ad

Most agencies run a single, generic ad and hope it lands. We build one creative per pain for two reasons. First, a sharp, specific worry stops the right person mid-scroll in a way that "we do site selection" never will. Second, running all five together tells us which pain actually pulls registrations, so we put the budget behind the winner instead of guessing.

Who each ad is built for

The audience stays constant across the set: the executives who own contact center and back-office operations (VPs of Operations and Customer Experience, Heads of Site Strategy, COOs at enterprises, and delivery leaders at BPO firms). What changes is the pain, and therefore which person in that audience each ad pulls forward. That is what a coordinated funnel looks like before a single dollar goes to ad management.
