Is This For You?

Who this training is for (and who it isn't)

We kept this session focused, so it lands hardest for the right people. See where you fit before you reserve a seat.

This is for you if…

  • ✓You own or influence where your contact center or back-office operations get located.
  • ✓You run, or are planning, a large agent or back-office workforce (hundreds to thousands of seats).
  • ✓You are weighing a new site, an expansion, or an onshore, nearshore, or offshore move.
  • ✓You have felt a market that could not staff, or attrition that would not stop.
  • ✓You want a decision grounded in labor data, not a broker's available inventory.

This is NOT for you if…

  • ✕You are choosing a single small office with no real hiring volume.
  • ✕You just want a broker to show you buildings and quote rents.
  • ✕You are not willing to look at labor, cost, and risk together.
  • ✕You have no say in location or workforce strategy.
  • ✕You are simply price-shopping a lease and nothing more.

Questions

Before you register

Why did I receive this invitation?

This session was built for a specific group: the leaders who own contact center and back-office location and workforce decisions. If you are here, you fit that profile. Seats are kept limited so the room stays small enough for live Q&A.

Who is this for?

VPs of Operations and Customer Experience, Heads of Site Strategy, COOs, and the BPO delivery leaders who decide where agent and back-office work gets done.

We already work with a broker or advisor. Is this still worth my time?

Especially then. Most brokers are paid on the lease, which means labor reality can take a back seat to the deal. This session shows you what an independent, labor-first read looks like, so you can judge whether the advice you are getting is aligned to your operation.

What happens if we just keep choosing sites the way we do now?

Probably nothing dramatic this quarter. But a market that quietly runs dry, attrition that compounds, and wage drift that erases the business case all add up, year after year. The point of this session is to show you those risks while there is still time to design around them.

Should I bring my team?

Please do. The leaders who get the most out of it bring the operations, finance, and site-strategy people who own the decision together, so everyone leaves on the same page.

What if I can't attend live?

Register anyway. We will send a recording to everyone who signs up, so you can watch whenever it is convenient. The live Q&A, though, only happens once.

How long is it?

About 45 minutes of training, plus live Q&A at the end.

What happens at the end?

You will get the full framework, yours to use however you like. If you would like help applying it to your own footprint, you will have the option to book a working session with the team. Either way, you walk away ahead.
