The Thinking Behind These Creatives

Our Approach

We don't point one ad at everyone and hope. Each creative is built for a different slice of your market, sorted by how aware that person already is of the opportunity, so the message meets them exactly where they are. Here is how the five fit together.

Reaching Your Whole Market

Problem Unaware

Savers who feel uneasy about the market but have never heard that retirement money can move into gold at all. Ad 01, The Door That Was Always Open makes the hidden possibility concrete and gets them to look.

Problem Aware

Savers who already fear a crash or inflation eroding what they built. Ad 02, When Paper Falls names the fragility of an all-paper nest egg and the pull toward something tangible.

Solution Aware

Savers who like the idea but are stuck on an objection. Ad 03, The Penalty That Isn't There removes the tax-and-penalty fear; Ad 04, It Was Never Your Job removes the it-sounds-too-complicated fear.

Most Aware

Savers ready to act but wary of who to trust. Ad 05, Educated, Never Sold leads with the education-first posture and the proof (2,500+ families, all 50 states) that separates Cedar from the late-night-ad crowd.

Why One Pain Per Ad

Most agencies run a single, generic ad ("invest in gold") and hope it lands. We build one creative per pain for two reasons. First, a sharp, specific worry stops the right person mid-scroll in a way that a generic gold pitch never will. Second, running all five together tells us which pain actually pulls registrations, so the budget goes behind the proven winner instead of a guess.

Who each ad is built for

The audience stays constant across the set: pre-retirees roughly age 50 to 65 with meaningful savings in a 401(k), IRA, or TSP, most of it sitting in paper assets. What changes is the pain, and therefore which person in that audience each ad pulls forward. Every ad sends to the same registration page for one free live training, so five different worries all funnel into one clean next step.
