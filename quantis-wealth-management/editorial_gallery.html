The Thinking Behind These Creatives

Our Approach

We don't point one ad at everyone and hope. Each creative is built for a different slice of your market, sorted by how aware that person already is of the problem, so the message meets them exactly where they are. Here is how the five fit together.

Reaching Your Whole Market

Problem Unaware

Affluent households who feel vaguely uneasy but have not named the leak. Ad 02, The Silent Tax Leak makes the invisible cost of uncoordinated withdrawals concrete and gets them to look.

Problem Aware

People already living the frustration. Ad 01, The Unpaid Project Manager speaks to those tired of relaying messages between their advisor and CPA; Ad 03, Enough On Paper speaks to those who cannot tell if they can truly afford to retire.

Solution Aware

People ready to act and weighing how. Ad 04, The 4am Question reaches those afraid of outliving their money in a shaky market; Ad 05, A Legacy, Not A Mess reaches those who want their estate and tax plan to finally agree.

Why One Pain Per Ad

Most firms run a single, generic ad and hope it lands. We build one creative per pain for two reasons. First, a sharp, specific worry stops the right person mid-scroll in a way that "integrated wealth and tax planning" never will. Second, running all five together tells us which pain actually pulls registrations, so the budget goes behind the winner instead of a guess.

Who each ad is built for

The audience stays constant across the set: affluent Northern Virginia and greater DC households within roughly 5 to 10 years of retirement, typically $2M and up in investable assets, often former executives or business owners making the decision as a couple. What changes is the pain, and therefore which person in that audience each ad pulls forward. That is what a coordinated funnel looks like before a single dollar goes to ad management.
