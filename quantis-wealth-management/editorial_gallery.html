The Thinking Behind These Creatives

Our Approach

We don't point one ad at everyone and hope. Each creative is built for a different slice of your market, sorted by how aware that person already is of the problem, so the message meets them exactly where they are. Here is how the five fit together.

Reaching Your Whole Market

Problem Unaware

Affluent retirees who feel scattered and worn down by their own finances but have never named the cause. Ad 01, The Lonely Quarterback shows them the problem: they have quietly become the middleman between advisors who never talk.

Problem Aware

Retirees already worried about a specific threat to their plan. Ad 02, The Silent Leak names the tax drain; Ad 03, Permission To Retire names the "can we actually retire" fear; Ad 04, The 4am Question names the fear of outliving the money.

Solution Aware

Retirees who know they need a real plan and are thinking about how to lock it in. Ad 05, Leave A Legacy reaches those focused on what they pass on, and on getting the estate, taxes, and investments to finally agree.

Why One Pain Per Ad

Most agencies run a single, generic ad and hope it lands. We build one creative per pain for two reasons. First, a sharp, specific worry stops the right person mid-scroll in a way that "we do retirement planning" never will. Second, running all five together tells us which pain actually pulls registrations, so we put the budget behind the winner instead of guessing.

Who each ad is built for

The audience stays constant across the set (affluent Northern Virginia pre-retirees and retirees, roughly ages 55 to 70 with $2M or more). What changes is the pain, and therefore which person in that audience each ad pulls forward. That is what a coordinated funnel looks like before a single dollar goes to ad management.
