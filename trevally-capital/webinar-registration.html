Sound Familiar?

The five worries keeping private-credit allocators up at night

If any of these hit home, this training was built for you. We tackle all five, in plain terms, with no fund pitch and no jargon.

🔁

One trade, five times

You spread private credit across managers, then read the holdings: every fund owns the same corporate direct loans. The diversification is an illusion, and it all reprices on one afternoon.

📉

The vanishing premium

The double-digit yields that justified locking client capital away for years keep grinding lower, while you still carry the illiquidity and the default risk.

🔦

Flying blind on managers

New funds keep landing on your desk, and you're underwriting first-time managers with a spreadsheet and a gut read. One blow-up costs you the client's trust.

⬛

Priced by the seller

Your largest alt position is marked quarterly, by the manager, on loans that never trade. You can't stress-test a black box priced by the people who sold it.

🔒

The seven-year bet

A closed-end fund locks capital into one vintage for years. Mistime the entry and you're trapped, watching the next vintage capture the better spreads.

Is This For You?

Who this training is for (and who it isn't)

We kept this session focused, so it lands hardest for the right people. See where you fit before you reserve a seat.

This is for you if…

  • ✓You are an RIA, family office, or wealth manager allocating client capital to private markets.
  • ✓You already hold private credit and want real diversification beyond corporate direct lending.
  • ✓You allocate on behalf of accredited investors or qualified purchasers.
  • ✓You want an asset class you can actually model and exit, not a quarterly mark you can't verify.

Questions

Before you register

Why did I receive this invitation?

This session was built for a specific group: RIAs and family-office allocators with a real private-markets program who are rethinking how they hold credit. If you are here, you fit that profile. Seats are kept limited so the room stays small enough for live Q&A.

Who is this for?

RIA founders and CIOs, family-office investment leads, and wealth managers allocating accredited or qualified-purchaser capital into private markets, who want a sharper read on where credit is mispriced.

I already allocate to private credit. Is this still worth my time?

Especially then. Most allocators have spread "private credit" across several funds that quietly all hold the same corporate direct loans. This session shows you what genuine diversification in credit looks like, so you can judge for yourself whether your sleeve is as spread out as you think.

Is this a pitch for a fund?

No. It is a training on how to think about mispricing, liquidity, and manager diligence in credit. The framework is yours to use however you like. If you want to look at how it applies to your own book, you can request a working call with the team. Either way, you walk away with the framework.

What happens if I keep allocating the way I do?

Probably nothing dramatic this quarter. But a crowded trade, compressing spreads, and marks you can't verify compound quietly. The point of this session is to show you those exposures while there is still time to reposition.

Can I bring a colleague or my investment committee?

Please do. The allocators who get the most out of it attend with the people they make these decisions with, so everyone leaves on the same page.

What if I can't attend live?

Register anyway. We will send a recording to everyone who signs up. The live Q&A, though, only happens once.

How long is it?

About 45 minutes of training, plus live Q&A at the end.
