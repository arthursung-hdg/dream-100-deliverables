The Thinking Behind These Creatives

Our Approach

We don't point one ad at every allocator and hope. Each ad is built for a different slice of your market, sorted by how aware that person already is of the problem, so the message meets them exactly where they are. Here is how the five fit together.

Reaching Your Whole Market

Doesn't See The Problem Yet

Allocators who own the popular funds and think they're set. Ad 01, Everyone Owns The Same Thing shows them they're in the same crowd as everyone else, and gets them to look closer.

Feels The Problem

Allocators already frustrated. Ad 02, Paying More, Getting Less names the shrinking returns; Ad 05, Locked In For Years names the money they can't touch when they need it.

Looking For A Better Way

Allocators actively shopping. Ad 03, Betting On Strangers reaches those unsure how to judge a new manager; Ad 04, What's It Really Worth? reaches those who can't trust the price on what they hold.

Why One Pain Per Ad

Most firms run one generic ad and hope it lands. We build one ad per frustration for two reasons. First, a single, specific frustration stops the right person mid-scroll in a way that "we manage mortgage securities" never will. Second, running all five at once tells us which one actually pulls registrations, so we put the budget behind the winner instead of guessing.

Who each ad is built for

The audience stays the same across all five: the people who allocate capital, RIA founders and CIOs and family-office investment leads, managing roughly $100M to $2B for high-net-worth and institutional clients. What changes is the frustration, and therefore which of them each ad pulls forward. That is what a coordinated funnel looks like before a single dollar goes to running the ads.
