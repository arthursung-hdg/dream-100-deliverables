01 · What winning looks like The Metric We're Accountable To

Sell the number, not the panel.

Most display conversations start with the screen — its size, its resolution, its specs. But the operators you want to win don't buy panels; they buy outcomes. The single biggest lever in this entire program is that shift: from selling the panel to selling the number the buyer already owns.

The instinct — where most of the market operates

The panel.

Screen size · resolution · brightness · the spec sheet. A conversation about the product.

The lever — what the buyer actually owns

The number.

Foot traffic · basket size · SKU velocity · finance attach. A conversation about their results.

The single metric this program is accountable to

Qualified sales conversations engaged, and meetings booked, for your commercial-display teams across LATAM, MEA, and Asia.

When we report to you each week, this is what we report against. And one honest distinction, up front: we commit to the process that produces meetings — a disciplined loop of testing and learning — not to a guaranteed conversion rate in markets where success depends on local nuance. The next section explains why that distinction is a strength, not a hedge.

03 · An honest foundation Candor, Up Front

What we'll own, and won't promise.

We've built these technology-driven outbound motions primarily in North America and Canada. The plays we run are well-established there. They are not widely run in the markets you're asking us to win — LATAM, the Middle East, and Asia. That cuts two ways, and we want both on the table.

The honest concern

We can't promise a message or offer performs in these markets the way it would where we operate intimately. Cultural receptiveness, channel behavior, and buying rhythms differ — sometimes sharply. Anyone who guarantees breakout results in unfamiliar territory is selling certainty they don't have.

The honest advantage

That same scarcity is why this capability is valuable. Very few partners are building sophisticated Clay-powered GTM motions aimed specifically at these regions. You are early to something most of your competitors aren't doing at all.

This admission shapes the entire engagement rather than weakening it — because we designed the delivery model around exactly this reality:

We own — what travels across any market

The machine.

Finding the right accounts, enriching them, building the targeting engine. Done-for-you, identical everywhere, because none of it depends on local nuance.

You lead — where local nuance decides

The market truth.

The message, the offer, the live conversation. Your in-market teams have the fluency we don't — and they work the warm leads the machine produces.

One motion, owned together

No context-switching between vendors, no one to blame across a handoff. What we guarantee is the engine and the discipline: a fast, structured experimentation loop that finds what resonates, market by market.

Earning the reply, then reaching them.

4.2 · Message-market-fit engineering — your choice

A message that lands isn't a clever line. It's the right pain spoken to the right operator, paired with an offer that's easy to say yes to — and together those two are the message. We engineer them as one unit, tuned per sub-niche. Your product range becomes ammunition: storefront displays as a foot-traffic lever, aisle signage to move slow SKUs, checkout screens as average-transaction lift, transparent OLED to spotlight premium items, centralized control as promo speed-to-market, and the retail-media layer as a revenue stream, not a cost. Because we can't guarantee which message wins in each market, we don't sell one — we build an experimentation system so the data tells us what resonates.

Advisory

We advise the likely pains and the lead-magnet offers most likely to land per segment, and hand your team the thinking to run with.

Hands-on

A full operating playbook of pain + offer combinations your in-market reps can start pitching immediately — built to deploy and test across markets.

Your market database won't come from a single source — and that's the most important decision we make on your behalf. Nearly every off-the-shelf database is built on US and Western European data, and coverage thins out sharply in exactly the markets you care about. So we treat each region as needing its own best-in-class source, blended and cross-verified through Clay — which is built to draw from many providers in sequence, filling the gaps any single one leaves.

GCC specialists

Asia

Local, by sub-market

The most fragmented region — local specialists by sub-market, with particular care for Japan and Korea, where low LinkedIn penetration and local-language records defeat most Western databases entirely.

Sourced & negotiated on your behalf — at cost

Our only incentive is the best coverage per dollar — because we profit from our work, not from your data.

What you receive: a structured, segmented, deduplicated market map — accounts organized by sub-niche, scored for buying signals, enriched contacts — sourced from the best-fit providers for each region, and owned by you.

06 · How we operate  /  08 · Compliance Rhythm & Risk, By Design

A sustainable operating rhythm.

A weekly working session

We suggest Mondays — align on direction and priorities for the week.

A written progress update

End of each week (Thursday/Friday) so you always have a clear, documented view of what moved — without daily-reporting overhead that slows the build.

If a phase needs tighter visibility, we step the cadence up for that stretch. Throughout, we work consultatively — reverse-engineering each workflow with your team so you understand why it's built the way it is. The goal is that your team grows more capable through this engagement, not more dependent on us.

Keeping it compliant

Outreach rules vary significantly across the regions in scope, and we treat that as a design input, not an afterthought.

Compliance is built into the motion

Automated LinkedIn outreach carries different platform and legal tolerance country by country — which is exactly why our Hands-on outreach is scoped to lower-legal-risk markets, with coaching everywhere else. Commercial messaging in most LATAM markets requires express consent and a clear opt-out — which our opt-in funnel satisfies by design, because every lead has actively raised their hand. And we map each market against its data-protection regime (Brazil's LGPD, Mexico's LFPDPPP, the Gulf frameworks) before scaling — built safe at scale, not retrofitted later.
