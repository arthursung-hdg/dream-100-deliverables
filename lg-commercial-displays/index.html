01 · The Winning GTM Philosophy

Sell the number, not the panel.

Most display conversations start with the screen: its size, its resolution, its specs. But the operators you want to win don't buy panels; they buy outcomes. The single biggest lever in this entire program is that shift: from selling the panel to selling the number the buyer already owns.

The instinct: where most of the market operates

The panel.

Screen size · resolution · brightness · the spec sheet. A conversation about the product.

The lever: what the buyer actually owns

The number.

Foot traffic · basket size · SKU velocity · finance attach. A conversation about their results.

The single metric this program is accountable to

Qualified sales conversations engaged, and meetings booked, for your commercial-display teams across LATAM, MEA, and Asia.

When we report to you each week, this is what we report against. And one honest distinction, up front: we commit to the process that produces meetings, a disciplined loop of testing and learning, not to a guaranteed conversion rate in markets where success depends on local nuance.

The next section explains why that distinction is a strength, not a hedge.

A Candid Analysis of Challenges and Opportunities.

We've built these technology-driven outbound motions primarily in North America and Canada. The plays we run are well established there in the overall business environment. LATAM, EMEA, and APAC, respectively, have different business environments and cultural reciprocity to sales. This presents us with both potential bottlenecks as well as opportunities.

Potential bottleneck

We can't promise a message or offer performs in these markets the way that it would where we operate intimately. Cultural receptiveness, channel behavior, and buying rhythms differ, sometimes sharply.

Potential opportunity

That same scarcity is why this capability can become extremely valuable. Very few businesses are building sophisticated Clay-powered GTM motions aimed specifically at these regions. We are early to something that most of our competitors aren't doing at all.

This reality dictates the nature of our partnership, because it truly becomes a partnered effort.

04 · The GTM Motion Structure (Data Enrichment)

TAM Data, Sourced Region by Region.

Based on our research, your market database won't come from a single source, and that's the most important decision we make on your behalf.

Nearly every off-the-shelf database is built on US and Western European data, and coverage thins out sharply in exactly the LATAM, MEA, and APAC markets we care about.

So we treat each region as needing its own best-in-class source, blended and cross-verified through Clay, which is built to draw from many providers in sequence, filling the gaps any single one leaves.

Latin America

Brazil & Mexico-led

Strongest accessible coverage in Brazil and Mexico, supplemented for the Andean and Southern Cone markets, with email-pattern sourcing for Spanish- and Portuguese-language companies.

Middle East & Africa

GCC specialists

Regional sourcing depth in the UAE, Saudi, and beyond that global databases simply don't prioritize, layered with verified-quality sources for the highest-value Gulf accounts.

Asia

Local, by sub-market

The most fragmented region: local specialists by sub-market, with particular care for Japan and Korea, where low LinkedIn penetration and local-language records defeat most Western databases entirely.

Sourced & negotiated on your behalf, at cost

The data itself is an asset you purchase and own; we don't mark it up or bake it into our fee. Part of the foundation fee buys our going to the right vendors per region, sample-testing them for accuracy before committing, and negotiating the most cost-effective stack for your targets.

What you receive: a structured, segmented, deduplicated market map: accounts organized by sub-niche, scored for buying signals, enriched contacts, sourced from the best-fit providers for each region, and owned by you.

4.2 · Message-market-fit engineering

Message = Pain + Offer.

In the noise-filled outbound market of 2026, clever tactics and gimmicky personalization is rapidly losing power. It's a full-circle moment, where success yet again relies on the fundamentals.

It's the right pain spoken to the right operator, paired with an offer that's easy to say yes to, and together those two are the message. We engineer them as one unit, tuned per sub-niche.

Your product range becomes ammunition: storefront displays as a foot-traffic lever, aisle signage to move slow SKUs, checkout screens as average-transaction lift, transparent OLED to spotlight premium items, centralized control as promo speed-to-market, and the retail-media layer as a revenue stream, not a cost.

06 · How We Operate & Compliance

Our Sustainable Operating Cadence.

A weekly meeting session

We suggest Mondays (KST) to review the past week's progress and align on priorities for the week.

A written progress update

End of each week (Thursday/Friday) so you always have a clear, documented view of what moved, without daily-reporting overhead that slows the build.

If a phase needs tighter visibility, we step the cadence up for that stretch. Throughout, we work consultatively, reverse-engineering each workflow with your team so you understand why it's built the way it is.

The goal is that your team grows more capable through this engagement, not more dependent on us.

Keeping it compliant

Outreach rules vary significantly across the regions in scope, and we treat that as a design input, not an afterthought.

Compliance is built into the motion

Automated LinkedIn outreach carries different platform and legal tolerance country by country, which is exactly why our Hands-on outreach is scoped to lower-legal-risk markets, with coaching everywhere else.

Commercial messaging in most LATAM markets requires express consent and a clear opt-out, which is why we suggested an opt-in funnel layer, because the compliance aspect is satisfied by design.

We will approach each market against its data-protection laws (Brazil's LGPD, Mexico's LFPDPPP, the Gulf frameworks) and always tread on the safer side before scaling.
